My name is Poompol Buathong, and I am currently a Ph.D. candidate in Applied Mathematics at the [Center for Applied Mathematics](https://cam.cornell.edu/), Cornell University, USA. I hold a Bachelor’s degree in Mathematics (2017) and a Master’s degree in Applied Mathematics (2020) from [Mahidol University](https://science.mahidol.ac.th/index.php), Thailand, as well as a second Master’s degree in Applied Mathematics (2024) from [Cornell University](https://www.cornell.edu/).

I am a recipient of the Development and Promotion of Science and Technology Talents Project (DPST) scholarship, awarded by the [Institute for the Promotion of Teaching Science and Technology (IPST)](https://www.ipst.ac.th/ipsten), Ministry of Education, Thailand. This prestigious scholarship has provided full financial support for my studies from undergraduate through doctoral level.

My research interests lie in operations research, kernel methods, and machine learning. I am particularly focused on developing novel Bayesian optimization algorithms, including those designed for set-valued input functions and those capable of handling objective functions structured as networks of functions, with support for partial evaluations. I am also interested in applying machine learning to solve real-world problems, with applications spanning food science, materials design, and chemistry-related domains.
