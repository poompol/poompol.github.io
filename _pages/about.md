My name is Poompol (Paul) Buathong, and I am a Ph.D. candidate in Applied Mathematics at the [Center for Applied Mathematics](https://cam.cornell.edu/), Cornell University, USA, where I work with [Prof. Peter Frazier](https://people.orie.cornell.edu/pfrazier/) on Bayesian optimization and AI for Science.

I received a Bachelor’s degree in Mathematics (2017) and a Master’s degree in Applied Mathematics (2020) from [Mahidol University](https://mahidol.ac.th), Thailand, where my thesis focused on Bayesian optimization for set-valued input functions under the supervision of [Assoc. Prof. Tipaluck Krityakierne](https://sites.google.com/view/tipaluck/), with co-supervision from [Prof. David Ginsbourger](https://ginsbourger.github.io) (University of Bern) during my internship. I also obtained a second Master’s degree in Applied Mathematics (2024) from [Cornell University](https://www.cornell.edu/).

I am a recipient of the DPST scholarship from [Institute for the Promotion of Teaching Science and Technology (IPST)](https://www.ipst.ac.th/ipsten), Thailand, supporting my studies from undergraduate through doctoral levels.

My research focuses on Bayesian optimization, machine learning, and their applications to scientific problems, including food science, materials design, and chemistry.
